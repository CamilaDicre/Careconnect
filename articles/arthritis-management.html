Effective strategies for living better with arthritis and maintaining mobility

üîç What is arthritis?

Arthritis is an inflammation of the joints that causes pain, stiffness, and swelling. It's very common in seniors and can significantly affect quality of life if not managed properly.

Main types of arthritis:

  • Osteoarthritis: The most common type, caused by cartilage wear
  • Rheumatoid arthritis: Autoimmune disease that affects multiple joints
  • Psoriatic arthritis: Associated with skin psoriasis
  • Gout: Caused by uric acid buildup in joints

Common symptoms

Joint pain, morning stiffness lasting more than 30 minutes, swelling, reduced range of motion, and general fatigue.

Regular exercise is fundamental for maintaining flexibility and strength in joints:

Recommended activities:

  • Walking: Low-impact exercise that strengthens muscles
  • Swimming: Excellent for joint mobility without pressure
  • Tai Chi: Improves balance and reduces stress
  • Gentle yoga: Increases flexibility and reduces stiffness
  • Cycling: Good cardiovascular exercise for joints

Swimming and water aerobics reduce joint load while providing resistance.

üí™ Resistance exercises

Light weights or resistance bands help strengthen muscles around joints.

Exercise precautions

Stop if you feel sharp pain, avoid high-impact exercises during inflammatory flare-ups, and always warm up before exercising.

ü•ó Nutrition for arthritis

A healthy diet can help reduce inflammation and improve joint health:

Anti-inflammatory foods:

  • Fatty fish: Salmon, tuna, sardines (rich in omega-3)
  • Berries: Blueberries, strawberries, raspberries
  • Leafy greens: Spinach, kale, broccoli
  • Nuts and seeds: Walnuts, almonds, chia seeds
  • Olive oil: Extra virgin olive oil
  • Turmeric: Natural anti-inflammatory

Foods to avoid:

  • Processed foods and refined sugars
  • Red meat and fried foods
  • Excess salt and alcohol
  • Dairy products (for some people)

Hydration tip

Drink plenty of water throughout the day. Proper hydration helps maintain joint lubrication and reduces stiffness.

üíä Medical treatments

There are various medical treatments available for arthritis:

Medications:

  • Pain relievers: Acetaminophen, ibuprofen
  • Anti-inflammatory drugs: NSAIDs for inflammation
  • Disease-modifying drugs: For rheumatoid arthritis
  • Topical creams: For localized pain relief

Physical therapy:

A physical therapist can design a personalized exercise program and teach proper movement techniques.

Alternative treatments:

  • Acupuncture: May help with pain relief
  • Massage therapy: Reduces muscle tension
  • Heat and cold therapy: Reduces pain and inflammation

Important

Always consult your doctor before starting any new treatment or medication. Never stop taking prescribed medications without medical supervision.

üè† Lifestyle modifications

Small changes in daily life can make a big difference:

Home adaptations:

  • Use ergonomic tools and utensils
  • Install grab bars in bathrooms
  • Use a raised toilet seat if needed
  • Wear supportive, comfortable shoes
  • Use assistive devices when necessary

Stress management:

Stress can worsen arthritis symptoms. Practice relaxation techniques like meditation, deep breathing, or gentle yoga.

Sleep quality:

Good sleep is essential for healing. Use supportive pillows and maintain a regular sleep schedule.

üìû When to contact your doctor

It's important to contact your doctor in the following situations:

  • Severe pain that doesn't improve with rest
  • Joint swelling that lasts more than a few days
  • Difficulty performing daily activities
  • Fever or other signs of infection
  • Side effects from medications
  • New symptoms or worsening of existing ones
