Stomach Ache

Acetaminophen

Use: Pain relief and fever reduction.
Description: Commonly used for mild to moderate stomach pain and discomfort. Should be taken as directed by a physician.

Pepto-Bismol

Use: Upset stomach, nausea, diarrhea.
Description: Helps relieve symptoms of indigestion, nausea, and diarrhea. Not recommended for children under 12 years.

Hypertension

Lisinopril

Use: High blood pressure.
Description: ACE inhibitor used to treat hypertension and heart failure. Should be taken daily as prescribed.

Amlodipine

Use: High blood pressure, chest pain.
Description: Calcium channel blocker that helps relax blood vessels. Take as directed by a doctor.

Headache

Ibuprofen

Use: Pain relief, inflammation.
Description: Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) used for headache, muscle aches, and fever. Take with food to avoid stomach upset.

Aspirin

Use: Pain relief, fever, inflammation.
Description: Commonly used for mild to moderate headaches. Not recommended for children with viral infections.

Colds

Acetaminophen

Use: Fever and mild pain.
Description: Helps reduce fever and relieve minor aches and pains associated with colds.

Dextromethorphan

Use: Cough suppressant.
Description: Used to relieve cough caused by colds or flu. Should not be used in children under 4 years.

Gastritis

Omeprazole

Use: Acid reflux, gastritis.
Description: Proton pump inhibitor that reduces stomach acid. Commonly prescribed for gastritis and ulcers.

Ranitidine

Use: Acid reduction.
Description: H2 blocker that helps decrease stomach acid. Used for short-term relief of gastritis symptoms.

Joint Pain

Naproxen

Use: Pain relief, inflammation.
Description: NSAID used for joint pain and inflammation. Take with food to avoid stomach upset.

Diclofenac

Use: Pain relief, inflammation.
Description: NSAID used to treat pain and inflammation in joints, muscles, and bones.
